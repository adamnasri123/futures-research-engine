# Market Atlas — ES Futures, June 2024 – June 2026

A systematic catalogue of every measurable pattern in our data (513 trading days of
24h 5-minute bars, 520 days of 1-minute bars), built 2026-06-11. Every effect is
reported with sample size and t-statistic; with ~12 studies × many buckets, a |t|>2
appears by luck ~5% of the time per bucket, so the **promotion bar is |t| ≥ 3, an
economically meaningful effect size, and a sensible mechanism**. Generated by
`backtest/atlas.py`; candidate trade tests in `backtest/candidates.py`.

## What is REAL in this data (passed the bar)

### 1. Intraday momentum at the 30min–2h horizon — t = 5.0 to 8.2
Serial correlation of k-bar RTH returns: +0.027 (30min), +0.050 (60min), +0.066 (2h).
By far the most significant *directional* structure found. Mechanism: institutional
order-flow autocorrelation / slow position building.
**BUT — tested as a trade (`candidates.py`), it cannot pay retail costs.** The
conditional edge per event is ~0.5–0.9 pts gross; our round-turn cost is ~0.62 pts
($3.12). All threshold variants land PF 0.87–0.97 after costs. A bank trading at
near-zero cost can harvest this; a $3-per-trade retail account cannot. *This single
number — edge-per-event vs cost-per-trade — explains most of this project's NO-GOs.*

### 2. Volatility clustering — t ≈ 13.5
corr(today's range, tomorrow's) = 0.60. After a high-vol day the next day averages an
80-pt range vs 50 after quiet days. Not directional, but the strongest fact we have.
**Uses:** (a) per-trade dollar-risk caps are *predictably* needed after big days (the
June 10–11 ~$1,000 stops were forecastable); (b) any strategy stats should be
vol-regime-conditioned; (c) crash-regime days are knowably different — day filters
keyed on prior-day range have a real basis.

### 3. Session range expansion (structural)
The afternoon breaks one side of the first-hour range on ~65%/58% of days; both sides
on only 25% (36% after narrow mornings — narrow AM = chop warning). This is why
breakout systems *feel* right — one-side continuation is near-certain — but which
side, after costs, has never beaten random in any of our tests.

## What looked promising and FAILED the trade test (same day, honestly)

### Gap fade — the atlas's best-looking directional read
10–25 pt overnight gaps faded intraday by ~7 pts on average (t=2.3, n=163); 90% of
small gaps filled same-day. Translated into an actual trade (fade at 9:35 open,
target prior close, multiple stop variants): **full-period +$536 at best (PF 1.07,
t=0.4), OOS ≈ $0, and far below the random-sign control's p95 (~$1,700–2,200).**
Wider gaps (25–60) faded LOSE outright (PF 0.63) — that atlas bucket (n=57) was
noise. Gap fade = NO-GO.

## What is NOISE (measured and rejected — do not revisit without new reason)

| Effect | Result |
|---|---|
| Prior-day high "liquidity" touches | 1h-later drift +0.65 pts, t=0.55. Fade 41% vs continue 46%. **Levels are not magnets or walls here.** |
| Day-of-week | Mon +11.9 (t=2.4), Wed +13.9 (t=2.0) — classic multiple-testing bait at 5 buckets; below bar. |
| Daily streaks / yesterday's direction | all t < 1.4 |
| Close-location (close near high → next day) | t ≈ 1.1 |
| Last-hour momentum-into-close | t ≈ 0.1–0.3 |

| 1-min structure / stop-and-reverse (separate study) | gross expectancy $0.00; pure cost bleed |

## The honest synthesis

Across 14+ strategy families, 9 instruments, 3 timeframes, and now a systematic
pattern atlas, the same shape repeats: **the market has real, measurable structure
(momentum at hour scale, vol clustering, range expansion) — but every simple
translation of it into trades pays more in costs than the structure yields.** The
effects are harvested by participants with ~100× lower costs and ~1000× faster
reaction. Retail edge, if it exists for us, must come from: (a) NOT trading (day
selection, risk caps — proven useful), (b) much lower frequency (weekly+ holds where
a 0.6-pt cost is trivial vs a 50-pt target), or (c) information beyond price history.

## Addendum (2026-07-14): externally-sourced candidates, computed

A sourced web-research sweep identified the two strongest published survivors; both
were tested same-day (`backtest/meanrev.py`, `backtest/noise_area.py`):

- **Multi-day tail mean reversion** (Baltussen et al., JFE 2019): the mechanism is
  **confirmed alive** on 1980–2026 daily data — index serial dependence is negative in
  every era since 1995, strongest recently (2020–24 lag-1 AC −0.18, t=−6.4). The
  long-tail trade is positive OOS (+0.29%/trade, 78% win) but its timing alpha over
  drift-matched random longs is small, and EOD-marked drawdowns **breach a $2,000
  trailing MLL even at 1 micro**. Real effect, incompatible with prop-eval risk limits.

  *Prop-rule survivability grid (added 2026-07-15, `meanrev.py` PART 3, 2016–2026):*
  TopStep-style $2k trailing = DEAD at every size; Apex-style $2.5k real-time trailing
  = DEAD; swing-style $3k EOD-trailing survives at 1 micro (+$7.8k/decade net); $4k
  static survives at 1–2 micros (+$15.7k at 2). So the strategy is only compatible
  with swing-permissive accounts with ≥$3k EOD-based drawdown — and even there, the
  random-long control caveat stands (drift capture, unproven timing alpha).
- **Noise-area intraday momentum** (Zarattini et al. 2024): fixed-rule replication on
  our 1-min window is **negative** (PF 0.85–0.89; the churn variant's entire loss ≈
  costs). Consistent with the effect living in high-vol regimes and institutional cost
  structures.
